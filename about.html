Bhojan Tatva

Tatva means essence or the value which is intrinsic to the subject. “Bhojan” (hindi word meaning food) Tatva embodies the dishes or preparations which add value to human body and mind…keeps it healthy and thriving at all times…gives it the energy to sustain and the freshness to renew itself.

Bhojan Tatva will present dishes which are long forgotten or are not as well known. It will bring to you preparations which are easy and quick to prepare. These preparations will be healthy and tasty. Most of these dishes are vegetarian.

We will start with a simple prayer for food which appears in the Vedas:

Vadani kaval gheta naam ghya shree hariche |

Sahaj havan hote naam gheta phukache |

Jivan kari jivitva anna he purna bramha |

Udar bharan nohe janije yadnya karma ||

Simply translated it means, “Oh Lord! I offer this food and taking your name I pray to you to grant me energy and well being. Food is the complete universe in itself and I will know this as I partake of it.”

Bhojan Tatva has been compiled for Mandakini Moghe, who realizes the importance of the above Shloka and even at the age of 83 she strives to influence everyone to partake of food which is healthy and tasteful. Here she shares recipes which she has read about or which has been handed down to her by her mother, mother-in-law and friends. She acknowledges everyone who has contributed to her knowledge of cooking.

The best part is she has prepared these recipes herself. So, they are not experimental. They are for real! Enjoy!
